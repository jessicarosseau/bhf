---
title: Blueberry Hill Muffins
description: Tall, tender muffins with a crackly sugar top and a berry in every bite — the one we make first with each new bucket.
category: breakfast
prepTime: 15 min
cookTime: 25 min
totalTime: 40 min
servings: 12 muffins
difficulty: Easy
featured: true
order: 1
ingredients:
  - 2½ cups all-purpose flour
  - 1 tablespoon baking powder
  - ½ teaspoon salt
  - ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  - 1 cup granulated sugar
  - 2 large eggs
  - 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  - ½ cup whole milk
  - ½ cup sour cream or plain yogurt
  - 2 cups fresh blueberries
  - 2 tablespoons coarse sugar, for topping
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 375°F. Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
  - Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt in a bowl.
  - In a large bowl, beat the butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy, about 3 minutes. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then the vanilla.
  - Stir in half the flour mixture, then the milk and sour cream, then the rest of the flour — mix just until combined.
  - Toss the blueberries with a spoonful of the flour (this keeps them from sinking), then fold them in gently.
  - Divide the batter among the cups and sprinkle with coarse sugar. Bake 22–26 minutes, until golden and springy. Cool 10 minutes before eating.
notes:
  - Frozen berries work too — fold them in straight from the freezer so they don't streak the batter.
  - A pinch of lemon zest in the sugar brightens everything up.
---

These are the muffins we reach for the morning after picking. The sour cream keeps them moist for days — if they last that long.
