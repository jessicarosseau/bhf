---
title: Old-Fashioned Blueberry Pie
description: A double-crust pie that lets the berries do the talking — barely sweetened, gently thickened, deeply blue.
category: family-favorites
prepTime: 30 min
cookTime: 55 min
totalTime: 1 hr 25 min
servings: One 9-inch pie
difficulty: Medium
featured: true
order: 2
ingredients:
  - Pastry for a double-crust 9-inch pie (your favorite, or store-bought)
  - 6 cups fresh blueberries
  - ⅔ cup granulated sugar
  - ¼ cup cornstarch
  - 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  - 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  - ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  - Pinch of salt
  - 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces
  - 1 egg, beaten, for the top crust
  - Coarse sugar, for sprinkling
instructions:
  - Heat the oven to 400°F. Roll out the bottom crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie plate.
  - In a large bowl, gently toss the blueberries with the sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, zest, cinnamon, and salt.
  - Pour the filling into the crust and dot with the butter.
  - Top with the second crust (whole with vents, or a lattice). Crimp the edges, brush with beaten egg, and sprinkle with coarse sugar.
  - Bake at 400°F for 20 minutes, then reduce to 375°F and bake 35–40 minutes more, until the crust is golden and the filling bubbles thickly.
  - Cool at least 3 hours before slicing — this is the hardest and most important step.
notes:
  - Set the pie on a rimmed baking sheet to catch any bubbling juices.
  - Full cooling lets the filling set so it slices cleanly instead of running.
---

Every family has its pie. This is a good starting point — swap in your own crust and make it yours.
